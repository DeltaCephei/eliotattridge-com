I do enormous amounts of thinking that quietly dies. Some of it dies in Tana, some in Obsidian, most in chats with an AI that I never come back to. None of it has a home that's mine, public, and built for thinking instead of for finishing.

The Pursue Learning blog is the wrong shape for that. It points at teachers, it has an audience, and a half-formed thought doesn't earn a place there. It's a place for things I've worked out enough to recommend.

So this site. The point is not to publish. The point is to have somewhere I'll happily write down a half-formed thought because the friction is lower than refining it into something else. Notes here are allowed to change. They're allowed to be wrong, briefly. They're allowed to sit at three sentences for a year and then suddenly become twelve paragraphs because something clicked.

The cathartic part matters more than I expected.

## What this isn't

It isn't a blog, in the way most blogs are linear and reverse-chron and anchored to dates. The dates are here so I can find things, not because the value of a thought decays with time. A note from last March about confidence-weighted multiple choice is just as relevant as one I wrote last week.

It isn't a wiki either. A wiki implies someone has worked things out. These haven't been worked out. That's the whole point.

It's a thinking space. Maggie Appleton's phrase. I'm borrowing it, and her philosophy, without borrowing her illustrations.

## The success metric

In two weeks, has this site been the reason at least three notes exist that wouldn't otherwise? If yes, the friction is right. If no, the answer isn't more features, the answer is finding what's slowing me down and removing it.
